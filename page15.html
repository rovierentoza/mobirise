Business Fair

(February 22, 2018)

Pasig City Science High School celebrated the month of February by showing their love for food which we call “Senior Business Fair.” All sections in grade 12 and grade 9 managed their own restaurants and activities with applying their knowledge in Economics. Each group has their own unique strategies and themes to attract customers.

Grade 12

G12-Anjana came up with a theme of Pinoy Heroes that is why they name their restaurants as “Kapitanjana.” Their restaurants sells rice meals and various snacks which most Filipino enjoys. In their booth customers enjoyed their carnival theme and enjoyed different games.


G12-Aphelion named their restaurant as “Level Aph” and sold budget-friendly merienda like fried sumo, pizza roll, and rice meals. They have a theme of different types of games such as mobile games, board games, and card games. They even managed a Mobile Legends Tournament where the winning would receive a one thousand pesos cash!


“Eclipsis” was owned by G12-Eclipse where they serve you different drinks such as shakes, neon drinks, iced coffee and lemonade. Their booth is a bar themed where you can enjoy your drinks while dancing or listening to the beat of the songs.


For a house escape game, “House of Equinox” of G12-Equinox is the perfect booth to experience a challenging game. They also serve food and drinks such as blue lemonade, milktea, fries, mojos and rice meals.



“Nowbrewz” served various food such as pastries, fried food with dips, rice meals and iced beverages. In G12-Nowruz, room 205, have the talented performers and bands in Pasig City Science High School like Alas Singko, Error 404, Blank, and many solo performers and collaborations. Their booth is where good food meets good music.



G12-Songkran named their restaurant as “Song-Eats” where in their booth customers enjoyed card games and the biggest karaoke house in school with many list of hit songs. Their restaurants also served rice meals, drinks, combo meals and snacks.

Grade 9

In G9-Chromium’s restaurant, customers love their street food in the menu and their special mango graham. They named their restaurant as “Chito Merienda” with the tagline “Tama na yan! Kainan na!” they did also sang harana hit songs to attract customers.


“Food.Day” is as restaurant owned by G9-Gallium that serves different types of snacks, coolers, and especially the sweets that could make you throwback on your childhood which are the gummies! They sold gummy bears, gummy worms, and gummy bananas. In their booth, customers enjoyed their movie booth and the dedication booth for students who have confessions or shoutouts.



G9-Lithium named their restaurant as “Lutong Ina.” It is where pascians enjoyed their ihaw-ihaw and flavored shakes. They also served chicken skin and mojos. They also managed a tekken tournament in their booth.


G9-Natrium’s “Habumbay,” didn’t managed any themed booth but they served student budget-friendly merienda subach as French fries, mango shake, gulaman, and burger that students really enjoyed.

G9-Platinum’s “BIXBI” restaurant, they served affordable prices and great deals of various food like sinangag meals, coffee jelly, blue lemonade and skewers. They also have instagramable spots in their booth for customers who love to take instagramworthy pics.

In G9-Radium, they named their restaurant as “HI-B0 (HILIG AT BUSOG)” where they served shakes, fried and dips, and their sweet drinks. They called their booth as Momoland which inspired from the one of the booth of last school year’s foundation day which was Happy Hour.

In G9-Vanadium, they named their restaurant as “Mango-go x Foopoon” because they have two themes. For Foopoon menu, they have rice meals and drinks. While for Mango-go menu, they have their diffirent types of specialty of mangoes such as shakes, mango float, mango graham and many more. They also held a kpop random dance competition and a kpop museum.
